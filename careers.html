Our Training Programme

A structured progression path that takes you from newcomer to top performer — the Wolf Training Programme.

Epsilon (0–3 months)
In-house training for newcomers. Build foundational knowledge, pass licensing exams, and learn the basics of financial advisory.
Alpha (4–8 months)
Alpha ONE and Alpha Sales 2 Success programs. Develop prospecting skills, client presentation techniques, and closing strategies.
Beta (9 months – 2 years)
Personal branding and seminar selling. Build your professional identity and learn to engage larger audiences effectively.
Gamma (3+ years)
Advanced level — becoming top salespeople. Master complex financial strategies and transition into leadership and mentorship roles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about joining

Do I need a finance background?
Not at all. We provide comprehensive training from scratch — product knowledge, client management, prospecting, and presentation skills. Many of our top advisors came from completely unrelated fields.
Is this just a sales job?
No. Financial advisory is about holistic planning — understanding each client's life situation and building strategies around their real needs. You're a trusted advisor, not a salesperson.
How much can I earn?
There's no income ceiling. You earn based on performance — a combination of active income and passive, recurring income that grows over time. Monthly earnings may vary, but are usually much higher than a regular salaried position.
Is this a full-time role?
Yes, but with full flexibility. You manage your own schedule — no fixed office hours, no micromanagement. Most advisors structure their time around their lifestyle and personal commitments.
What certifications do I need?
You'll need to pass pre-contract licensing exams (CMFAS M5, M9, M9A, HI). We provide in-house tutorials, sample papers, exam clinics, and 1-to-1 coaching to make sure you're fully prepared.
Can I advance into management?
Absolutely. There's a proven career advancement pathway that lets you fast-track into management — where your primary role shifts to identifying, mentoring, and developing the next generation of advisors.
