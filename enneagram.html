What is the Enneagram

An ancient map of human nature

Nine patterns. One of them is quietly running your life.

The Enneagram is a system of nine personality types — each one a distinct pattern of attention, motivation, and fear. Its roots trace back through centuries of contemplative traditions, refined in the twentieth century into the modern psychological framework used today by therapists, leadership coaches, and serious students of the self.

Unlike tests that describe what you do, the Enneagram reveals why you do it. It names the unconscious strategy you adopted early in life to feel safe, loved, or significant — a strategy so habitual you've likely mistaken it for your personality itself.

You will have one dominant type. You'll also have a "wing" (a neighbouring flavour), specific directions you move in under stress and in growth, and an instinctual subtype that shades everything. Knowing your type won't change who you are — but it will show you the shape of the prison you've been living inside, and the door you didn't know was unlocked.

The Nine Types

Which of these is you?

Read them honestly. Something usually flinches in recognition.
1
The Reformer
Principled · Purposeful
Driven by a vision of how things ought to be. Holds themselves, and the world, to a standard that rarely sleeps.
2
The Helper
Warm · Perceptive
Senses what others need before they name it. Finds belonging through giving — and struggles to receive.
3
The Achiever
Driven · Adaptive
Reads the room, becomes what the room rewards. Terrified of failure and of being seen not succeeding.
4
The Individualist
Expressive · Introspective
Feels fundamentally different from others. Drawn to depth, beauty, and longing — suspicious of the ordinary.
5
The Investigator
Perceptive · Private
Retreats into the mind to stay safe. Protects energy fiercely; trades intimacy for understanding.
6
The Loyalist
Vigilant · Committed
Scans constantly for what could go wrong. Fiercely loyal, quietly anxious, unsure who to trust — sometimes including themselves.
7
The Enthusiast
Spontaneous · Versatile
Generates possibilities endlessly. Runs from pain into the next exciting thing — keeping all doors open as a way of life.
8
The Challenger
Powerful · Protective
Takes up space without apology. Protects the people they love, guards a tenderness no one is allowed to see.
9
The Peacemaker
Receptive · Reassuring
Merges with those around them, loses track of their own preferences. Keeps the peace — sometimes at the cost of their own aliveness.
